Cardiac Psychologist

        
Who are they?

A Cardiac Psychologist is a mental health expert who helps people with heart problems feel better mentally and emotionally.
Heart disease can bring a lot of stress, anxiety, and sadness, and that's where these psychologists step in.
They help patients manage these feelings, cope with the challenges of living with heart disease, and make healthier life choices.
        
What they do?




    
  • Help with Stress & Anxiety: If you’re feeling nervous or worried about your heart condition, they’re there to listen and offer support.
    • 

    
  • Support Recovery: After a heart  surgery or heart attack, they help people adjust and get back to a positive mindset.
    • 
    
    
  • Encourage Healthy Habits: They teach you how to manage stress, quit smoking, eat healthy, and live a better and comfortable life to limit 
  emergencies as a result of heart related diseases.
    • 

    

    
            
    What qualities do you need?
    


    

  • Understanding of Mental Health:They need strong knowledge of how mental health issues like anxiety and depression can affect heart health.
    • 


  • Compassion and Empathy:It's important to be kind and supportive, as patients may be going through emotional or physical stress.
    • 


  • Good Communication:They must explain thoughts, feelings, and treatment plans clearly and calmly.
    • 


  • Teamwork Skills:Cardiac psychologists work closely with doctors, nurses, and other professionals in a healthcare team.
    • 


  • Stress Management:They should be able to help patients manage stress—and stay calm themselves in busy medical environments.
    • 


  • Observation Skills:Being able to notice when a patient is struggling emotionally, even if they don't say it out loud, is very important.
    • 


  • Willingness to Learn:New treatments and research are always coming out, so staying up-to-date is key.
    • 


  • Motivational Support:Helping patients make positive lifestyle changes (like quitting smoking or exercising) takes encouragement and patience.
    • 

    

    
        
    What Qualifications do you need?
    


    

  • Good GCSEs, especially in English, Maths, and Science
    • 


  • A-levels or equivalent, often including Psychology or Biology
    • 
    

  • A degree in Psychology, approved by the British Psychological Society (BPS)
    • 
    

  • A postgraduate degree in Clinical Psychology (e.g. DClinPsy)
    • 
    

  • Clinical experience working with patients
    • 
    

  • Registration with HCPC to work as a clinical psychologist
    • 
    

  • Extra training or experience in heart health or health psychology
